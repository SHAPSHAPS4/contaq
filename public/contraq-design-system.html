Part 1

Design Principles

Five core principles that govern every design decision in Contraq. Each reflects the reality of M&E construction professionals who make high-stakes decisions under time pressure.

1

Clarity Over Cleverness

Every interface must communicate its purpose in under 3 seconds — no clever interactions that require learning.

Construction professionals switch between office and site throughout the day. They open Contraq between phone calls, in the van, during site walks. If a screen doesn't make sense immediately, they'll revert to their spreadsheet. Every second of confusion is a reason to churn.

Do
Upload Drawing PDF

Clear label, obvious icon, single action. The estimator knows exactly what will happen.

Don't
New

Ambiguous label — new what? New project? New quote? The user has to think. That's a failure.

2

Earned Trust

Trust is built through consistency, accuracy, and transparency — not through marketing language or decorative UI.

M&E subcontractors have been burned by software that over-promises. They trust their experience over any tool. Contraq earns trust by always showing where data came from, how confident the AI is, and never hiding uncertainty behind polish. A confident wrong answer is worse than an honest "I'm not sure."

Do
22mm copper pipe, LTHW flow Medium
Source: DWG-M-001 Rev C · Confidence: 72%

Shows confidence level, source drawing, and revision. The estimator can verify and override with context.

Don't
22mm copper pipe, LTHW flow AI VERIFIED

No source, no confidence score, and "AI VERIFIED" implies certainty that doesn't exist. Erodes trust fast.

3

Density With Breathing Room

Handle dense data without feeling cramped — generous spacing around dense content, not sparse content with wasted space.

Estimators work with 200-line takeoffs, multi-trade schedules, and cross-referenced specs. They need to see a lot of data at once to spot patterns and errors. But dense doesn't mean cluttered — the whitespace goes between sections, not between every row. Group related items tightly; separate groups generously.

Do
Mechanical — Pipework
22mm Cu pipe, LTHW 145m
28mm Cu pipe, LTHW 89m
35mm Cu pipe, CHW 67m
Mechanical — Fittings
22mm gate valve 24nr

Tight rows within groups, generous space between trade sections. Scannable and dense.

Don't
22mm Cu pipe, LTHW 145m
28mm Cu pipe, LTHW 89m
22mm gate valve 24nr

Equal spacing on every row, no grouping — looks clean but the estimator can't scan by trade. 3 items fill the screen.

4

Mobile Works On Site

Touch targets must be generous, contrast must survive direct sunlight, and text must be readable at arm's length with gloves on.

Engineers and site managers use Contraq on phones and tablets in plant rooms, on scaffolding, and in the rain. Interactions designed for a mouse at a desk fail in these conditions. If the site diary entry requires precision tapping or squinting, they'll write it on paper and forget to enter it later.

Do
Add Journal Entry
Approve Invoice

48px touch targets, high contrast icons, clear labels. Usable with gloves in bright sunlight.

Don't
Edit
Del
Dup
...

Tiny touch targets, low contrast, abbreviated labels. Impossible to use on site with cold hands.

5

Systematic Intelligence

AI capabilities must feel like a natural extension of professional judgment, not a magic trick — with clear, honest visual language for every AI state.

Estimators have decades of experience. They don't want AI that claims to replace their judgment — they want AI that saves them the tedious parts (counting, cross-referencing, flagging conflicts) so they can focus on the decisions. The AI must always be transparent about what it did, how confident it is, and where the human should take over.

Do
AI extracted 47 items · 38 high confidence · 9 need review
Processing time: 4.2s · Model: Claude · KB v7.2

Distinct AI colour (purple), clear count breakdown, processing metadata. Honest and transparent.

Don't
✨
AI Magic Complete!
Your takeoff is ready

Decorative gradient, emoji, "magic" language. No useful information. Feels like marketing, not a tool.

2C. Light Mode Surface Hierarchy

Six surface levels create visual depth and hierarchy. Higher levels sit "above" lower levels. Use consistently to communicate component relationships.

Level 0 — Page Background
#F5F7FA · --surface-page
Outermost background. Never for components.
Level 1 — Default Surface
#FFFFFF · --surface-default
Cards, panels, modals, sidebars.
Level 2 — Raised Surface
#F0F4F8 · --surface-raised
Table alt rows, input backgrounds, chips.
Level 3 — Sunken Surface
#E8ECEF · --surface-sunken
Code blocks, terminal panels, depressed inputs.
Level 4 — Overlay Surface
rgba(0,0,0,0.04) · --surface-overlay
Hover states over Level 1 surfaces.
Level 5 — Inverted Surface
#1E2433 · --surface-inverted
Dark panels, processing terminal, footer.

3C. Typography Rules

1
Minimum font size: 11px. Never smaller. Period.
Even captions and badges floor at 11px. On mobile in sunlight, anything smaller is unreadable. This is non-negotiable.
2
Line length: 45-75 characters for body text.
Never full width on wide screens. Use max-width on text containers. Optimal: 65 characters per line for reading comfort.
3
Heading hierarchy: never skip levels.
H1 → H2 → H3 in DOM order. Accessibility requirement (screen readers) and visual consistency. If you need a smaller heading, it's H3, not a styled H1.
4
Weight contrast: 2+ weight steps between heading and body in the same section.
H3 at 600 with body at 400 = 2 steps. This ensures clear visual hierarchy without relying on size alone, which matters at smaller scale differences.
5
All text must pass WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text).
Use var(--color-neutral-600) minimum on white backgrounds (7.5:1). Never use neutral-400 or lighter for body text.
6
Letter-spacing: only apply to CAPTION level (0.4px). Never to body text.
Tracking on body text reduces readability. The only exception is negative tracking on Display (-1.5px) and H1 (-0.5px) for visual density at large sizes.
7
Monospace for numbers: all quantities, references, and financial figures use JetBrains Mono.
This ensures columns align visually in tables and that reference numbers (DWG-M-001, QTE-2026-0047, £24,350.00) are instantly recognisable as data, not prose.
8
Responsive scale: max 20% reduction from desktop to mobile. Never below minimum readable size.
Display: 52→36px (31%). This is the only exception. All other levels stay within 20% (e.g. H1: 28→22px = 21%, acceptable). Body text does not change.

Interaction & Motion System

Every animation in Contraq is purposeful, fast, and accessible. No decoration for its own sake. Motion communicates state changes — processing, completion, error, attention.

6A. Motion Principles

Purposeful
Every animation communicates something. Processing: activity. Completion: success. Error: attention. Decoration for its own sake: never. If you can't explain what state change the animation communicates, remove it.
Fast
Construction professionals have no patience for slow animations. Micro-interactions: 100-150ms. Component transitions: 150-250ms. Page transitions: 200-350ms. Never exceed 400ms for any interface animation.
Accessible
All animations respect prefers-reduced-motion. Every @keyframes must have a reduced-motion alternative — typically instant state change with no transform.

7C. Icon Usage Rules

1
Icons never appear alone without an accessible label or tooltip.
Every icon-only button must have aria-label or an associated tooltip. Decorative icons use aria-hidden="true".
aria-label="Delete item"
Icon button with no label
2

Consistent spacing between icon and adjacent text label. Use gap: 8px in flex containers.
3
Never mix icon libraries in the same view.
Lucide only. No mixing with Heroicons, Feather, FontAwesome, or Material. Visual consistency is non-negotiable.
4
Interactive icons: minimum 44px × 44px touch target.
If the visual icon is 18px, pad the clickable area to 44px using padding or a ::after pseudo-element. This is an Apple HIG requirement and critical for site use.
44px target

5
Decorative icons: aria-hidden="true". Functional icons: aria-label.
Icons next to text labels are decorative (screen reader will read the text). Icon-only buttons are functional and need explicit labels. Get this wrong and you break accessibility.

10A. CSS Architecture

/public/
  contraq-tokens.css     ← CSS custom properties from tokens JSON
  contraq-base.css       ← Reset, base elements, typography scale
  contraq-components.css ← All 31 component classes
  contraq-utilities.css  ← Utility classes (spacing, text, flex, grid)
  contraq-animations.css ← All @keyframes definitions
  contraq-overrides.css  ← Page-specific overrides (import last)

Load order: tokens → base → components → utilities → animations → overrides

BEM Naming Convention

.component-name              /* Block    */
.component-name__element     /* Element  */
.component-name--modifier    /* Modifier */
.is-active, .is-loading      /* State    */
.has-error, .has-success      /* State    */

/* Examples */
.btn                          ← block
.btn__icon                    ← element
.btn--primary                 ← modifier (variant)
.btn--lg                      ← modifier (size)

10B. 20 Developer Rules

1
Always use CSS variables
Never hardcode colour values. Use var(--color-*).
2
Always use spacing tokens
Never use arbitrary px values. Use var(--space-*).
3
<button> for actions, <a> for navigation
Never use <div onclick>.
4
Never override disabled with pointer-events
Disabled means disabled. Don't hack around it.
5
Always provide loading states
Every async operation needs a visible loading indicator.
6
Semantic HTML before ARIA
Use <nav>, <dialog>, <table> first.
7
Test at 200% zoom
All layouts must remain usable at 200% browser zoom.
8
Never animate width or height
Use transform and opacity only. Width/height trigger layout recalc.
9
Debounce search inputs (300ms)
Don't fire on every keystroke. Wait for pause.
10
Handle empty states explicitly
Never show a blank panel. Always render an empty state component.
11
Touch targets: 44px minimum
Expand with padding or ::after if visually smaller.
12
No !important except utilities
Utility overrides only. Never in component CSS.
13
currentColor for icon SVGs
Icons inherit text colour. Never hardcode stroke/fill.
14
Always include focus styles
Never use outline: none without a replacement.
15
Validate on blur, not keystroke
Form validation triggers when the user leaves the field.
16
Respect prefers-reduced-motion
All @keyframes must have a reduced-motion alternative.
17
Use aria-live for dynamic content
Extractions, toasts, validation changes need live regions.
18
Text alt for icon-only buttons
Always add aria-label to icon-only buttons.
19
Never rely on colour alone
Always pair colour with text, icon, or pattern for state.
20
Use CSS variables from day one
Dark mode readiness. Every colour through a variable.
Part 11

Contraq UI Patterns

8 common interaction patterns specific to Contraq. Each pattern describes the problem, the solution, the key principle, and anti-patterns to avoid.

01AI Extraction Result
How to display AI-extracted data with confidence indicators, source attribution, flags, and human override capability.
Key principle
Always show provenance (where did this come from?) and confidence (how sure is the AI?). Every extracted item must trace back to a source drawing and revision.
Anti-patterns
Hiding confidence scores. Showing "AI Verified" without evidence. No way for estimator to override or correct. No source attribution.
02Progressive Pipeline
The multi-stage quote building workflow: Upload → Drawing Extraction → Spec Analysis → Takeoff Consolidation → Pricing.
Key principle
Always show where you are, what's complete, and what the next action is. Never dead-end the user. Every stage has a clear CTA to proceed or a blocker explaining why they can't.
Anti-patterns
Allowing skip to pricing without extraction. Showing all stages as clickable when prerequisites aren't met. No progress indication during long operations.
03Density Management
How to display 50+ line items in a table without overwhelming. Trade filtering, confidence filtering, column selection, row grouping by trade.
Key principle
Show everything when needed, focus when working. Default view shows grouped by trade with counts. Expand to see all items. Filter to isolate problems (low confidence only).
Anti-patterns
Paginating a 47-item takeoff. Hiding items behind a "Show more" that resets scroll. Equal row spacing without trade grouping.
04Empty → Populated Transition
How panels transition from empty state to populated state. The empty state must motivate action, not just inform about absence.
Key principle
Lead with the outcome ("Build your first AI quote"), not the absence ("No quotes found"). Always provide a clear first action. When data arrives, the empty state fades out and content fades in — no page reload.
Anti-patterns
Blank white panels. "No data" with no CTA. Empty table with just headers. Generic stock illustrations.
05Conflict Resolution
How to display conflicts between drawing and spec with clear resolution options. Drawing says 22mm, spec says 28mm — the estimator decides.
Key principle
Never hide conflicts. Surface them visually (warning border, conflict icon), contextualise them (show both sources), and give the estimator tools to resolve (pick A, pick B, enter manual value).
Anti-patterns
Silently picking one value. Flagging conflicts without showing both sources. Requiring a separate screen to resolve.
06AI State Communication
How to communicate AI processing stages (preparing, reading, extracting, validating, complete) without anxiety or impatience.
Key principle
Show progress, explain what is happening, manage expectations about timing. Use the processing terminal for transparency. Show token usage and KB version. Never say "please wait" — say what's happening now.
Anti-patterns
Opaque spinner with no context. "AI is thinking..." with no detail. Fake progress bars that don't reflect real progress. No indication of what stage the process is in.
07Financial Data Display
How to display monetary values, percentages, and totals consistently across the platform.
Key principle
Always monospace (JetBrains Mono). Always 2 decimal places for £. Right-aligned in tables. Positive = neutral colour. Negative = danger colour. Totals always visually prominent (bold, larger, separated).
Anti-patterns
Sans-serif for currency values. Left-aligned numbers in tables. Inconsistent decimal places. Green for positive (success green is for status, not money).
08Mobile Field Use
How key workflows adapt for phone/tablet on site: quick project status, journal entries, invoice approval, engineer cert checks.
Key principle
The most common on-site actions must require no more than 3 taps. Sidebar collapses. Tables become stacked cards. Touch targets expand to 48px+. High contrast for sunlight readability. Offline-tolerant where possible.
Anti-patterns
Desktop table on mobile (horizontal scroll). Tiny close buttons on modals. Multi-step forms that lose state on orientation change. Light grey text in sunlight.
